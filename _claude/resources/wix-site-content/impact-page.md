## Four Impact Pillars

### 1. Digital Literacy Training
Emergent Works programs equip system-impacted individuals with hands-on experience using industry-accepted digital tools to complete professional tasks efficiently and confidently. Participants report increased digital confidence, stronger problem-solving skills, and greater readiness for education and employment opportunities.

**Testimonial — "Stepping Out Of My Comfort Zone"**
> "This program motivated me to step out of my comfort zone in so many ways, and I gained useful knowledge and skill in Digital literacy that I did not have before."
> — Lakresha

### 2. Social-Emotional Learning
Emergent Works programs amplify participant voices through music production, audio engineering, studio recording, and guided mental health sessions. Participants build self-awareness, emotional regulation, confidence, and a stronger sense of identity while expressing their experiences and shaping their futures.

**Testimonial — "Break Down Any Barriers"**
> Before joining Emergent Works I was pretty stagnant with my music career. But when I joined the program I saw how I can use my creative side to open the doors to places I have never been to. The highlight of being in the program was learning how I can apply my artistry in a studio session and it really set foot in me. It created a confidence in me to break down any barriers and just go for it.
> — Julius

### 3. Career Development
Emergent Works programs prepare participants to navigate today's workplace by building foundational technical and social skills alongside real-world experience. Through hands-on training, mentorship, and applied learning opportunities, participants strengthen professionalism, communication, confidence, and career readiness, supporting long-term economic stability and growth.

**Testimonial — "The Skills To Move Forward"**
> This program has truly impacted my life by giving me the confidence to grow, the skills to move forward, and the support of a community that encouraged me every step of the way.
> — Zeek

### 4. Community Engagement
Emergent Works programs equip participants to collaborate, examine community issues, and lead change through civic engagement and research. Participants build critical thinking, communication, and leadership skills while using their voices and experiences to contribute to solutions that strengthen their communities.
